Song Vocal Syllable Transcriber

A deep learning pipeline for time-accurate Japanese song transcriptions.

As part of a three-person team, we tackled the challenge of creating high-quality, time-accurate subtitles for karaoke. The project involved building a model that could listen to a song's vocal track and precisely predict the start and end time of each syllable.

Technologies: Python, PyTorch, Transformers, Wav2Vec2, Rust

In our team of three, my primary responsibility was the foundational data pipeline. This was a critical, hands-on role that included:

  • Data Curation & Cleaning: I performed the entire data collection and cleaning process, personally curating a high-quality dataset from scratch by meticulously going through numerous Hololive VTuber music channels.
  • Data Pipeline Ownership: This process required exceptional attention to detail to prepare the raw data for our machine learning models, directly impacting the performance of the final project.

The project culminated in a research paper outlining the approach and findings. Our syllable prediction model achieved an F1 score of 0.535 across 110 Hiragana classes.

Key findings showed that many classification errors occurred between similar-sounding syllables (e.g., "wo" vs. "o"), a common challenge in vocal analysis due to pronunciation variations in singing.

Polysolve Calculator

An LLM-powered calculator for solving multi-step word problems.

In a two-person team, we created a novel calculator capable of parsing multi-step word problems and generating verifiable, step-by-step solutions. The system leverages a bipartite graph to map relationships between variables and equations.

Technologies: Python, Rust, Slurm, Large Language Models (LLMs)

My core contributions focused on the machine learning and research aspects of the project:

  • LLM Implementation: I utilized a Large Language Model to parse key information and relationships from unstructured word problems.
  • HPC Management: I was responsible for hosting and fine-tuning the model on WPI's Turing High Performance Computing (HPC) cluster, managing resources and jobs using Slurm.
  • SOTA Research: I conducted comparative research on the reasoning capabilities of State-Of-The-Art (SOTA) LLMs to benchmark our approach.
